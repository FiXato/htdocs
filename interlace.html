openMSX: How to emulate the interlaced display mode of the MSX standard

openMSX is alpha software and currently only intended for developers.

Interlacing (combined with alternate page display) was a method used in MSX to double the vertical resolution of a screen. A programmer would take two screen of normal resolution, and by instructing the VDP to alternate between these screens the optical effect of a higher resolution could be achieved. The effect was that the first page was displayed normally during the even screen refreshes, but during the odd screen refreshes the second page would be displayed but half a line lower, in between the lines of the previous picture so to speak. This resulted in a flickerry high resolution mode.

There are two methodes to emulate these high resolution screens. The first one is to really emulate the hardware behaviour and is shown below. Later on, I noticed that most browser can not handle gif animations with a speed of 50 frames a second, so please save the gif and use a good picture editor tool if your browser isn't fast enough!! Also if your monitor has a refresh rate of for instance 75Hz the effect of a 50 or 60Hz MSX screen refresh will not be viewed correctly, since you will see one of the two images for two screen refreshes on your monitor, making the image more flickery then on on The Real Thing(tm). Not to mention ofcourse that the afterglow of a modern monitor is much less then the old monitors/TV's used on an MSX thus increassing the flicker.


Most browsers will not refresh quickly enough between the two pictures. (It should be every 20 ms!!)
